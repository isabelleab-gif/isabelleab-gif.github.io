Isabelle's top five movie scenes

These movie scenes may not all be Oscar-worthy to the Academy, but they feel like peak cinema to me.

  1. Robin Williams' monologue in Good Will Hunting. This remains one of my favorite movies ever. Never thought I'd need to see Robin Williams as a therapist. (I have yet to see a bad Robin Williams movie.)
  2. Miranda Priestly's grand entrance in The Devil Wears Prada. I probably rewatch this movie 4-6 times per year. Meryl Streep is unsurprisingly iconic.
  3. Father Judd's Monologue in Wake Up Dead Man: A Knives Out Mystery I'd say this is my favorite movie of the year. I found Father Judd's portrayal of faith to be refreshing and I was so taken by the writing (after watching countless cash-grab, surface level movies recently).
  4. "How to Act Like a Man" from The Birdcage. They don't write comedies like this anymore. The Birdcage has become a movie night favorite in my friend group.
  5. The parade in Ferris Bueller's Day Off. I watched this movie as a high schooler and still recommend it to anyone who somehow hasn't seen it. This scene is just so much fun.
